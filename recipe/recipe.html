    • A Basic Fondue Recipe

    Ingredents

    • 1 cup dry white wine
    • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (or cornstarch)
    • ¼ teaspoon ground nutmeg
    • ½ pound shredded Swiss cheese
    • ½ pound shredded Gruyère cheese
    • ¼ teaspoon salt
    • 1 (1 pound) loaf French bread, cut into 1 inch cubes

    Tips:

    1. Cheese quality will affect Fondue. The best cheese to use are:
    Fontina, Gruyère, Gouda, Swiss, Cheddar, Comte, Emmentalar, Raclette, and Vacherin.
    2. It's easier to make Fondue if the cheese is grated rather than choped.
    3. Cornstarch is a nice gluten free option over flour and helps negate clumping.
    4. Wine directly affects the flavor profile of the Fondue so chose one you like!

    Directions

    1. Bring wine to a simmer in a fondue pot over medium-low.
    2. Stir in flour/cornstarch and nutmeg until well combined.
    3. Add Swiss and Gruyère cheeses, 1/4 pound at a time, mix until melted, then add more cheese.
      Repeat till you used up your cheese and season it with salt.
    4. Serve with cut-up French bread or whatever else you like!
